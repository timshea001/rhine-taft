Dream Transference Under Controlled Conditions

Volunteers read a short page late in the day and then slept in a dark, cool room. In the morning, each person wrote a few lines about any dream scene that stood out. Staff later checked for shared themes or images across the group.

Most nights gave no clear match. On rare nights a pair wrote down the same small scene, like a road at dusk or rain on a tin roof. The file holds scans of forms, time stamps, and hand notes. The study kept claims modest and left room for later work.
